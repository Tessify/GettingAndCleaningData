# Variables:
The input for the script are the following variables:
- activity label
- identifier of the subject who carried out the experiment.
The script uses the mean and standard deviation of the following variables:
- tBodyAcc-XYZ: time body acceleration in X, Y and Z directions
- tGravityAcc-XYZ: time gravity acceleration in X, Y and Z directions
- tBodyAccJerk-XYZ: time body acceleration jerk signal in X, Y and Z directions
- tBodyGyro-XYZ: time body gyroscope in X, Y and Z directions
- tBodyGyroJerk-XYZ: time body fyroscope jerk signal in X, Y and Z directions
- tBodyAccMag: time body acceleration magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- tGravityAccMag: time gravity acceleration magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- tBodyAccJerkMag: time body acceleration jerk signal magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- tBodyGyroMag: time body gyroscope magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- tBodyGyroJerkMag: time body gyroscope jerk signal magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- fBodyAcc-XYZ: Fast Fourier Transform body acceleration in X, Y and Z directions
- fBodyAccJerk-XYZ: Fast Fourier Transform body acceleration jerk signal in X, Y and Z directions
- fBodyGyro-XYZ: Fast Fourier Transform body gyrsoscope in X, Y and Z directions
- fBodyAccMag: Fast Fourier Transform body acceleration magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- fBodyAccJerkMag: Fast Fourier Transform body acceleration jerk signal magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- fBodyGyroMag: Fast Fourier Transform body gyroscope magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
- fBodyGyroJerkMag: Fast Fourier Transform body gyroscope jerk signal magnitude of the three-dimensional directions
